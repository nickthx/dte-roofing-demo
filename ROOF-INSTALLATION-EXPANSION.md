### 2. Roofing Material Selection for Columbus Homes (~900 words)

**Location:** Replaced brief material cards with comprehensive sections

**Introduction:**
- Central Ohio climate demands (temperature extremes, storms, winter ice, humidity)
- Balancing performance, aesthetics, longevity, and budget

#### Asphalt Shingles (Detailed Section)

**Advantages for Ohio Climate:**
- Cost-effective initial investment
- Wide color variety for Columbus architectural styles
- Proven performance in Midwest weather
- Easy to repair after storm damage
- Readily available replacement shingles

**Considerations:**
- Shorter lifespan (20-30 years)
- Less energy efficient
- Granule loss over time in sun exposure

**Best For:** Budget-conscious homeowners in Grove City and Columbus seeking reliable protection

#### Architectural Shingles (Detailed Section)

**Advantages for Ohio Climate:**
- Superior wind resistance (up to 130 mph) for severe storms
- Enhanced impact resistance against Central Ohio hail
- Dimensional appearance and depth
- Longer lifespan (30-50 years)
- Better warranties with algae resistance

**Considerations:**
- Higher upfront cost
- Heavier weight may require structural assessment on older homes

**Best For:** Columbus homeowners seeking premium appearance and storm protection

#### Metal Roofing (Detailed Section)

**Advantages for Ohio Climate:**
- Exceptional lifespan (50+ years)
- Superior energy efficiency for summer cooling
- Snow and ice slide off easily, reducing ice dams
- Extremely low maintenance
- Fire resistant and environmentally sustainable

**Considerations:**
- Highest initial investment
- Requires specialized installation expertise
- May not suit all Columbus neighborhood styles
- Can be noisier during heavy rain (attic insulation minimizes)

**Best For:** Long-term homeowners in Dublin and Hilliard prioritizing durability and energy savings

**Service References:** Links to gutter installation and siding services
